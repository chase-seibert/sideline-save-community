Recommended gear

Keep the action smooth—even when the play isn’t.

A phone gimbal uses small motors to steady your iPhone while you follow a player across the field or court. The result is calmer, easier-to-watch sports video with fewer distracting bumps and tilted horizons.

    

Why use a gimbal for sports?

Sports make you pan, stop, reframe, and move quickly. A gimbal helps those movements feel intentional instead of shaky.

01

Smoother tracking

Motorized stabilization reduces small hand movements while you follow a runner, drive, or fast transition.

02

More level framing

The gimbal helps keep the horizon steady when you turn, shift your stance, or move along the sideline.

03

Easier-to-watch clips

Controlled pans and fewer sudden jolts keep attention on the play, especially when highlights are viewed on a larger screen.

Better results on game day.

A gimbal helps, but good sports video still starts with positioning and deliberate camera movement.

  • Balance before powering on.Center the phone in the clamp and make sure it is secure before the game starts.
  • Start wider than you think.A little extra space around the player makes fast movement easier to follow without losing the play.
  • Pan with the action, not every fake.Use smooth body turns and let the gimbal soften your movement instead of chasing every small change.
  • Practice the controls first.Learn the follow mode, recenter action, and phone mounting at home so game time stays focused on the sport.
  • Charge both devices.Long events use the phone and gimbal batteries. Bring a battery pack if you expect a full day at the field.

Stay behind the line.

Never step into the playing area or block players, officials, or spectators to get a shot. Watch your surroundings, follow venue rules, and treat the highlight as less important than everyone’s safety.
