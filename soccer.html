The camera-roll problem

A goal lasts seconds. Recording the whole game lasts much longer.

With the built-in Camera app, you either start early and save long stretches of midfield play or wait and risk losing the pass, run, or turnover that created the chance.

Sideline Save lets you follow the game with the camera ready and decide later. When an attack moves toward goal, press record. If the buildup is still within your selected 5-, 10-, or 20-second window, the finished video begins with it. Keep recording through the shot, save, rebound, or celebration, then press stop.

Game-day setup

Frame the space, not only the ball.

Soccer develops across a wide field. A little context makes the eventual shot or save much easier to understand.

  • Start wide.Keep the ball carrier, likely passing option, and goal direction visible whenever your sideline position allows it.
  • Use 20 seconds for developing attacks.The Soccer preset starts there so the video can retain more of the buildup before you press record.
  • Keep recording through the reaction.The celebration, goalkeeper recovery, or teammates arriving often completes the story.
  • Stay outside the technical and playing areas.Follow club and venue rules and never block a coach, official, player, or spectator for a better angle.

Common questions

How soccer recording works.

Does Sideline Save record the whole soccer game?

No. While the app is open, it keeps a short rolling window ready. A video is saved when you press record, continue through the play, and press stop.

Can the video include the pass before I pressed record?

Yes, when the pass falls within the 5-, 10-, or 20-second window you selected and Sideline Save was open with the camera ready.

Why does the Soccer preset start at 20 seconds?

Soccer attacks often develop through several passes or a long run. The longer starting window keeps more context, and you can switch to 5 or 10 seconds whenever you prefer smaller videos.

Where does the finished video go?

Sideline Save saves finished videos locally to your Photos library and organizes them in its Saved Videos view. It does not upload your sports footage to a Sideline Save account.
