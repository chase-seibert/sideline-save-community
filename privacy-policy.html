About this policy

Sideline Save is developed and operated by Chase Seibert. This policy describes how the Sideline Save iOS app and its developer-operated analytics service handle information.

This policy does not govern Apple’s Photos or iCloud services, or third-party apps and services you choose when sharing a video. Those services have their own privacy practices.

Information handled on your device

Sideline Save processes the following information locally to provide its features:

  • Camera and microphone: Camera video and optional microphone audio are processed on your device to maintain the rolling buffer and create clips.
  • Photos: Videos you save remain in your Photos library. Sideline Save reads back the videos it created so you can browse, play, organize, share, favorite, or delete them.
  • Motion: Device motion is used only while the optional level meter is active. Motion readings are not stored or transmitted.
  • App settings and records: Sport presets, onboarding state, local capture statistics, saved-video references, and related clip metadata are stored in the app’s private container.
  • Recoverable buffers: Short-lived camera buffers may be kept in the app’s private container after an interruption. They expire after approximately five minutes and are subject to count and storage limits.
  • Temporary share files: Exports are removed after sharing, and files left by an interrupted share are removed after 24 hours. File modification times are read only to perform this cleanup.
Important: Sideline Save does not upload your camera feed, saved videos, audio, Photos asset identifiers, or saved-video record identifiers.

Analytics we collect

When analytics is enabled in a distributed build, Sideline Save may send limited product analytics over HTTPS to a developer-operated endpoint hosted on Amazon Web Services.

Apple categoryWhat Sideline Save sendsPurpose
Device IDA random identifier created for this installation of the app.Analytics; not linked to identity; not used for tracking.
Product InteractionApp opens, capture and Settings actions, selected options, saves, shares, deletions, counts, and event timestamps.Analytics; not linked to identity; not used for tracking.
Other Usage DataCamera, Microphone, and Photos permission states.Analytics; not linked to identity; not used for tracking.

Analytics also includes app version, platform, build audience, and technical event or batch identifiers needed to deliver and process the events.

Apple uses the broad “Device ID” label for the persistent random installation identifier. It is not an Apple advertising identifier, IDFV, hardware serial number, account ID, or contact identifier. It changes after the app is deleted and installed again.

Sideline Save does not send your name, email address, precise location, contacts, advertising identifier, hardware identifier, Photos content, video content, audio content, crash logs, performance measurements, or capture diagnostics as analytics.

How we use information

The limited analytics described above is used to:

  • measure app usage and understand whether features are useful;
  • understand permission and save flows, including where people encounter difficulty;
  • plan improvements and evaluate product changes; and
  • operate, secure, and troubleshoot the analytics service.

We do not use analytics for advertising, marketing profiles, credit decisions, or attempts to identify an anonymous installation.

Sharing and service providers

Sideline Save does not sell personal data. It does not share data for cross-app advertising or tracking, and it does not use data brokers.

Amazon Web Services provides infrastructure for the analytics endpoint. Service providers may process the limited information described here only to provide, protect, and maintain the service. They are required to protect it consistently with this policy and applicable requirements.

Internet infrastructure necessarily processes network information, such as an IP address, to deliver a request. Sideline Save does not place an IP address in its analytics event payload or use network information to build an identity or advertising profile.

When you intentionally share a clip using the iOS share sheet, the destination you choose receives the content you selected. Its handling of that content is governed by its own privacy policy.

Retention and deletion

Information on your device

App settings, saved-video references, local statistics, recoverable buffers, and queued analytics remain in the app’s private container until normal app cleanup removes them or you delete Sideline Save. Recoverable buffers expire after approximately five minutes. Temporary share files are removed after sharing or, if interrupted, after 24 hours.

Deleting Sideline Save removes its private on-device data and causes a future installation to create a new random installation identifier. Videos already saved to Photos remain there until you delete them in Sideline Save or the Photos app.

Uploaded analytics

Raw analytics is retained only as long as reasonably necessary to operate, secure, understand, and improve Sideline Save. Aggregated statistics that no longer identify an installation may be retained longer.

Your choices and rights

  • You can review or change Camera, Microphone, Motion, and Photos permissions in iOS Settings.
  • You can turn off the Level Meter in Sideline Save Settings to stop use of motion data for that feature.
  • You can delete saved clips from Sideline Save or Photos.
  • You can stop future analytics collection from an installation by deleting the app. This also removes queued, not-yet-uploaded analytics from the device.
  • You may contact the developer to ask questions or request access to, correction of, or deletion of applicable information.

Sideline Save has no user accounts and does not collect contact information in its analytics. As a result, the developer may not be able to connect a request to a particular installation. If you request deletion, include the approximate installation and usage period; reasonable efforts will be made to locate and delete matching raw records.

Children’s privacy

Sideline Save is intended to help parents, families, and other sideline supporters record sports highlights. It is not designed to collect names, contact details, account information, or the content of children’s videos or audio. If you believe information from a child was provided to the analytics service contrary to this policy, contact the developer so it can be investigated and deleted where possible.

Security

Sideline Save uses administrative and technical safeguards appropriate to the limited information it handles. Analytics is sent over HTTPS, requests are signed for service integrity, and the app avoids placing media or personal identifiers in analytics payloads. No method of storage or internet transmission can be guaranteed completely secure.

International processing

The analytics service is hosted in the United States. If you use Sideline Save from another country, the limited analytics described above may be processed in the United States, where privacy laws may differ from those in your location.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when Sideline Save’s features, service providers, or data practices change. The effective date at the top identifies the latest version. Material changes will be reflected here and, when appropriate, in the app or App Store listing.
